Grammar

This is where you will learn about the basics of Spanish Grammar:

  1. Nouns
    Nouns in Spanish are either masculine or feminine. This is indicated by the article (el or la) that is used with the noun, as well as by the ending of the noun itself. For example, the noun "el libro" (the book) is masculine, while "la mesa" (the table) is feminine.
  2. Adjectives
    Adjectives in Spanish must match the noun they are describing in gender and number. For example, "el perro grande" (the big dog), "la casa pequeña" (the small house).
  3. Verbs
    Spanish has a number of verb tenses, including present, past, and future. The most basic verb tense is the present tense, which is used to describe actions that are happening now. For example, "Hablo español" (I speak Spanish). Subject-verb agreement
  4. Subject-Verb Agreement
    In Spanish, the verb must agree with the subject in terms of number (singular or plural) and person (first, second, or third). For example, "Yo hablo español" (I speak Spanish), "Nosotros hablamos español" (We speak Spanish).

Ser Vs Estar

In Spanish, the verbs "ser" and "estar" both mean "to be," but they are used in different ways.

  1. Ser
    "Ser" is used to describe inherent qualities or characteristics, such as someone's identity or the nature of something. For example: Soy alta. (I am tall) or Él es de México. (He is from Mexico.)
  2. Estar
    "Estar" is used to describe a temporary state or condition. For example:Estoy cansada. (I am tired) or Ellos están enfermos. (They are sick.)

    3. Learn Basic Spanish Greetings

    In English you can say YOU to practically any person or group of people, whether you know them or not. In Spanish, however, there are many ways to say YOU, and they vary depending on the formality of the relationship between the speaker(s) and listener(s)

    1. Tu
      You should only use tú when talking to people you know well, especially those that are the same age as you or younger. Tú can be used to speak directly to children, close relatives, friends, peers, or pets. For example: ¿Vas tú a la fiesta esta noche? (Are you going to the party tonight?)
    2. Usted (Singular)
      Usted (often abbreviated Ud.) is used when addressing someone older, a person you do not know, a superior, or someone to whom you would like to show a lot of respect. For example: ¿Cómo se llama usted? (What is your name?)
    3. Vos (Singular)
      Vos is mainly used in a region of South America called the Southern Cone , which is made up of Argentina, Paraguay, Uruguay, and parts of Chile. It is also commonly used in Nicaragua and may also be heard in Guatemala, El Salvador, Honduras and Costa Rica. Many people use vos in place of both tú and usted. For example: ¿No querés vos más jugo? (You don’t want more juice?)
    4. Ustedes (Plural)
      Ustedes (often abbreviated Uds.) is used to address two or more people. For example: Señor Gonzalez y Doctor Encina, ¿quieren ustedes decir algo? (Mr. Gonzalez and Dr. Encina, do you want to say something?)
    5. Vosotros (Plural)
      Vosotros is primarily used in Spain to address a group of people with whom you are familiar. It is essentially the plural form of tú. For example: ¿Adónde vais vosotros esta noche? (Where are you (all) going tonight?)
